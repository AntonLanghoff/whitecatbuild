Dynamic Content

In Fader space you have recorded fixed lighting states: channels whose levels change only with the master that controls them.

In the docks you can also assign dynamic content: channels whose intensity may change independent of the position of the master.

Those channels may be coming from these sources within White Cat:

  • Chasers
  • Trichromy/Quadrichromy color wheel
  • Channels from video tracking aka Air-Lighting
  • etc etc...

But may also come from outside of White Cat:

  • a DMX lighting board coming as a Dmx-In, or an Art-Net signal received on the network, sent by other apps( Archaos, I-Phone, VVVV, Schwartzpeter, Freestyler, etc…) : see Dmx-IN and Art-Net IN

Those channels are not in a fixed state. Their intensity may vary, so they are called dynamic content.

Assigning dynamic content to a dock simply provides a link to this content.
