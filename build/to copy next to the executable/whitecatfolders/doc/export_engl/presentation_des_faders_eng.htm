Purpose of a Fader

The 48 Faders in White Cat are more than Masters.

The main difference from classic lighting board is that WhiteCat's Faders contains 6 docks.

Each dock may contain:

  • fixed lighting state (classical masters)
  • dynamic lighting states coming from WhiteCat internals ( chasers, trichromy, video tracking video, gridplayers, ... )
  • dynamic lighting states from outside DMX (dmx-in, art-net-in)
  • link to the level of ONE channel (direct channel)
  • audio commands (audio player)

Depending of selected dock, what is rendered by the fader is different. The fader outputs ONLY what is in the SELECTED DOCK. When you change of dock, you change the ooutput of the fader.

The output of the 48 faders are merged in HTP in a dedicated buffer, separated from CueList buffer. Levels coming from Faders buffer are printed to screen in orange color.

Each fader may be manipulated:

  • manually by mouse, or a midi controller
  • each fader may reeive a time for its LFO and can have fade properties  IN, OUT, delays in/out)
  • or be remoted internally  by Banger, or in Midi by a third party software

Other entries will help you to have a hand on Faders:

  • Arduino open-hardware
  • remote iCat for iOS devices ( iPhone/iPod, iPad )

There is two spaces dedicated to Faders in WhiteCat:

  • extended version, called Faders Space

You access to it by pressing [F10] or calling [Faders] in the Menu window

Fader Space enables a more precise work than minifaders.

  • a simplified, windowed version, called Minifaders

You can access to it by pressing simultanously [shift] [F10] ou clicking [MiniFaders] by the Menus window.

Minifaders are enabling a global vision of yourfaders, Lock Preset, All at Zero commands and the Fgroups.
