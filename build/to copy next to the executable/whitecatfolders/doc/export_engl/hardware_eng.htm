Hardware and Operating System

White Cat works with Windows XP, Vista, and Windows7.

For the moment the software is focused on traditional lighting.

It works with the following DMX devices:

-all interfaces with the Art-Net protocol (ethernet)

-Enttec Open (usb)

-Enttec PRO (usb)

-Velleman K8062D/VM116 (usb)

-Sunlite Siudi 5C-6C-8C (usb)

-Usbdmx512-online.net (usb)

Minimal Configuration

  • Without video-tracking (Air-Lighting): Laptop XP 1.7Ghz single-core RAM: 1024Mb video memory: 128Mb
  • With video-tracking (Air-Lighting): Laptop XP 1.7Ghz single-core RAM: 2048Mb video memory: 512Mb
  • Screen: 1024×600 is usable, but you will feel much more comfortable with a resolution of 1280×800, which is the original design size of the workspace.

1440×900 is the best because it enables you to see more windows.

Known problems on certain laptops:

  • graphic card limitations and a small L1 cache size may affect the operation of White Cat: you can encounter a lag with the mouse and screen refreshing. In this case, try updating your graphic card driver, and check to see if its 3D parameters can be tweaked to a performance mode. No trouble was reported using recent ATI / NVIDIA with 256Mb minimum.

Recommendations

Disable the sleep/hibernate mode of the computer because White Cat and usb dmx connections do not support it !

Personally I disable sleep on both the monitor and the HDD, in both battery and sector modes.

It's really best to install White Cat directly on the root directory of your HDD, directly under C:/ or D:/.

Testing a computer before buying

  • If you are thinking about buying a computer specifically to use with White Cat, it is possible to test it first:
    • copy the White Cat folder onto a usb flash drive
    • try White Cat in the store (if allowed) directly from this flash drive
    • pay special attention to latency in the mouse, especially with the Faders and MiniFaders windows ON. Have a look at the way calling channels by typing on the keyboard reacts. Be aware that the video tracking window is not a good test, as the cameras are usually at 640×480 resolution, which slows everything down (tracking must be done with a camera at 320×240).

Buying guide

  • pay special attention to processors. Certain processors have their performance strongly limited by battery life and portability considerations. Computers with long battery life are less prone to having a limited processor.
  • if you know how, take a look at the L1 and L2 caches in Bios. The bigger the number, the better it will be.

This test may be done with CPU-Z, a free tool you can copy to a usb flash drive (download the zip file, not the set-up version).

  • avoid net-books, as their resolution is very low, their performances being optimized for conventional usage (word processing, internet, … ) . The published performance is generally more a matter of luck than anything else.
  • look for a computer equipped with an ATI or NVIDIA graphic card that will play 3D games.

Light and Video on the same

Using VVVV and White Cat together on the same laptop is possible, but you will need at least a core i5 processor and a 512Mb graphic card. For price, Asus has 16 inch machines in a range from 800 to 1200 euros. ROG machines from Asus are actually my choice for a performance/price ratio. Generally, you will have to focus on machines made for gaming. But it's a rapidly changing market, and products are not the same quality from one year to the other, so the best thing to do is to discuss the topic on the forum or on a gamers' forum.
